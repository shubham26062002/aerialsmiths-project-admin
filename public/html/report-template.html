Quality Level (as per AS 5488.1:2019)

The majority of the data presented here is considered Quality Level B (QL-B) and physical verification is always advised. In some locations an approximate depth was unable to be gathered and these locations should be treated with caution.

QL-B provides relative subsurface feature locations in three dimensions. The minimum requirement for QL-B is relative spatial position, which can be achieved via an electromagnetic frequency locating device. An electronic lo- cation provided by a locator to QL-B standard has a maximum horizontal tolerance of ± 300 mm and a maximum vertical tolerance of ± 500 mm.

Underground Services Location Limitations

The purpose of Underground Services Location is to locate known services using plans as a guide, EMI (Electromagnetic Induction tools), GPR (Ground Penetrating Radar), manhole lifters, and visual observation.

  • Without directly observing a pipe or cable, we cannot always identify what the service is.
  • Due to the inherent nature of the ground (e.g. how saturated it may be, the type of substrate or abundance of sand or high electro-magnetic geology) it is possible that some services may not be locatable.
  • Without directly observing a pipe or cable, we cannot always identify what the service is.
  • Where services are congested or the material, depth, and size of the service is incompatible with our locating tools, it is not always possible to confidently assess the location or existence of these services.
  • Sometimes a cable may be too small to be picked up by the GPR.
  • Where fibre is laid on its own, it cannot be located with cable locators or GPR.
  • Abandoned and/or unmapped services and anomalies may be identified during the investigation.
  • It is possible that a strong conductor may mask the presence of other services that are poor conductors, e.g. a high voltage cable will likely make it difficult to detect other lower voltage cables, pipes, etc.
  • Our marking onsite/reporting represents our best assessment of the underground services, but due to the subjective nature of service locating, we take no responsibility for any inaccuracies or misinterpretations.
